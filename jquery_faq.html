Top 10 Questions for Blåfjella–Skjækerfjella National Park

Where is Blåfjella–Skjækerfjella National Park located?
In Trøndelag county, central Norway, and spans the municipalities of Verdal, Snåsa, Grong, Lierne, and Steinkjer
How big is Blåfjella–Skjækerfjella National Park?
The park covers 1,924 km2 (743 mi2) of land
What wildlife can you expect to see here?
A wide variety including Artic fox, brown bears, roe and red deer, moose, lynx, and wolverines
What plantlife is common here?
Blåfjella–Skjækerfjella is famous for its spruce trees as this is the first place they were introduced to in Norway. The park is full of old-growth forests with the soil being very condusive to plant life.
What does Blåfjella–Skjækerfjella mean?
The park was given this name in 2004. The name is likely derived from Sami names for the area, and roughly means Shafts of Blue Mountains
How does this park rank compared to other parks?
While every park is unique, we consider Blåfjella–Skjækerfjella truly special. It is also the third-largest park on the Norwegian mainland
What are the names of reserves attached to the park?
There are four reserves surrounding Blåfjella–Skjækerfjella: Arvasslia Nature Reserve, Berglimyra and Klumplifjellet Nature Reserve, Gaundalsmyra Nature Reserve, and Storfloa Nature Reserve
What kind of terrain can you expect?
The park contains fields of wildflowers, alpine forests, and lakes
What activities are allowed in the park?
This park is popular with hikers as well as avid fishers and hunters
Is there an entrance fee?
No, these parks are open to all visitors
